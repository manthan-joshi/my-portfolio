CodeM4

Solubilization and purification of M4 membrane protein.

Undergraduate thesis at Tranalab, Centre for Human Genetics, Bangalore. Optimized the expression and purification of a stubborn recombinant membrane protein domain, and won first rank in the 2019 batch for it.

Imagine trying to work with a protein so stubborn it refuses to dissolve. That was my thesis. M4 is a recombinant membrane protein domain, and the existing protocols weren't getting enough of it out of inclusion bodies in a usable form.

The problem

M4 is a membrane protein domain with roles in immune response modulation, cell adhesion, and signaling. It has therapeutic and diagnostic relevance. But membrane proteins are a pain to purify. Expression in E. coli pushes the protein into inclusion bodies, insoluble aggregates that require aggressive solubilization and refolding to recover functional protein.

Previous protocols got low yields and incomplete solubilization. The thesis was an attempt to fix that.

What I did

  • Prepared competent E. coli BL21(DE3) cells and transformed them with the M4-containing plasmid
  • Induced protein expression with IPTG (0.5 to 1 mM), then harvested and sonicated to release inclusion bodies
  • Ran two different solubilization conditions in parallel. Condition 1 used 8M urea and 20mM imidazole. Condition 2 added 1% Triton X-100 and 1% Tween 20 on top
  • Purified solubilized protein via Ni-NTA affinity chromatography with stepwise urea washes (6M, 4M, 2M) followed by 500mM imidazole elution
  • Dialyzed eluted fractions against 20mM Tris / 20mM NaCl to remove imidazole and exchange buffer
  • Tested refolding by sugar affinity chromatography under three different elution conditions
  • Quantified protein yield at A280 using Nanodrop and validated purity with SDS-PAGE

The finding

Adding Triton X-100 and Tween 20 to the urea-based solubilization buffer materially improved yield. Condition 2 gave an A280 of 0.68, versus 0.46 for Condition 1 alone. That translated to roughly 2 μM of purified M4 under Condition 2 versus 1.37 μM under Condition 1, a meaningful improvement for downstream characterization work.

Translating A280 to protein concentration using ε = 95,965 M-1cm-1: Condition 1 yielded ~1.37 μM (47.95 ng/μL), Condition 2 yielded ~2.03 μM (71.05 ng/μL). A ~48% improvement from detergent addition alone.

The result that felt most real was also the most mundane: adding the right detergent got the protein out. Not every good science finding is glamorous.

What didn't work

The sugar affinity chromatography for refolding assessment was stubborn. Even with 1M sugar in the elution buffer, M4 wouldn't release cleanly from the resin. This is documented honestly in the thesis as a deviation from expected results, with a proposed follow-up on alternative elution strategies.
