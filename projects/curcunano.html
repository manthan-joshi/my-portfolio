CurcuNano Therapeutics, a pitch for MRSA.

Team pitch project for the Biotech Enterprise course. A curcumin-loaded lipid nanoparticle platform targeting refractory methicillin-resistant Staphylococcus aureus. Class project, not a real startup.

The assignment was to build an investor-grade pitch for a plant-based antibiotic alternative. We picked MRSA because the unmet need is real, the resistance problem is urgent, and curcumin has serious published data behind it.

The unmet need

MRSA infects roughly 80,000 patients per year in the US, causing around 11,000 deaths. Vancomycin is still the standard of care, but vancomycin-resistant strains (VRSA) are emerging, and no new class of Gram-positive antibiotic has been approved in over a decade. The WHO projects antimicrobial resistance will cause 10 million deaths annually by 2050.

Curcumin has demonstrated antimicrobial activity against MRSA in the literature but has terrible bioavailability on its own. Nanoparticle delivery is the obvious next step.

What I owned

  • Led the Pipeline and Growth Story workstream, responsible for the therapeutic pipeline, differentiation strategy, and long-term growth narrative
  • Built the slide and speaker notes for the Funding Ask, anchoring our valuation and milestone plan
  • Kept six other workstreams consistent with each other, making sure the market slide, the science slide, and the financial slide didn't contradict one another by the end of the deck
  • Contributed to the full pitch across unmet need, competitive landscape, IP strategy, financial projections, and commercialization roadmap
The hard part of a pitch team isn't the slides. It's making sure slide 12 doesn't accidentally undercut slide 4.

Platform thinking

The class project framing was single-indication, but we built the narrative around a platform. MRSA first, because of the clarity of the need and the strength of the curcumin data. Then expand across the WHO critical pathogen priority list, using the same chitosan-PEG nanoparticle chassis with targeted modifications for different bacterial envelopes.

Phase I
Refractory MRSA

Lead indication. Hospital-acquired and post-surgical infections. Chitosan-PEG LNP with curcumin payload.

Phase II
Gram-positive expansion

Extend to VRSA and vancomycin-resistant enterococci. Same chassis, validated payload, new preclinical work.

Phase III
WHO priority list

Targeted envelope modifications for Gram-negative coverage. Longer horizon, bigger market, larger platform claim.
